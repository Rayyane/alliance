About Alliance

Alliance Pediatrics' primary goal is to provide high quality, evidence-based treatments and training opportunities to our clients. We believe individualized attention and a collaborative relationship are necessary components of solution-based care.

Our aim is to create a trusting, reliable and caring environment for our patients and their families. We strive to provide our clients with the skills they need to continue to grow and develop personally and professionally.

Our family services include the assessment of presenting concerns and a comprehensive treatment plan to address identified issues. Treatment plans are always tailored to meet the needs of the client. However they frequently include individual therapy for the child as well as caregiver training. In addition, we provide families with oversight of home therapy programs, school consultations and collaboration with other care-providers.

Currently, we serve many different clients. Some of our clients include children, adolescents, caregivers, related service professionals (occupational therapists, physical therapists, speech-language pathologists, psychologists, etc), therapists seeking professional development (BCBA certification, psychology licensure) and school staff.

We work with professionals in a variety of ways. Some of these services include training seminars for direct care staff and school personnel, clinical case consultation and supervision opportunities.

Individual Therapy

An individualized approach is necessary in assessing and treating presenting concerns.We utilize research-based approaches when determining the best treatment plan. For example, we focus on a behavioral approach to assess and design interventions to decrease challenging behaviors and to increase skill acquisition. A behavioral approach allows the clinician to analyze the variables that occur before and following a behavior. Following a careful assessment, individualized interventions are created to address these variables. Individualized interventions are carefully monitored and adjusted until changes are seen in the behaviors of concern.
Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is also a common approach utilized in therapy. Through CBT, the therapist assists the individual to address the thoughts, feelings and behaviors associated with presenting concerns. Following a CBT model, the clinician, individual and caregivers work together to create an individualized treatment pan. A CBT approach involves identifying objective goals and systematically working towards those goals with the client, their caregivers and other professionals when appropriate.

Family Therapy and Caregiver Training

Caregiver involvement is a primary component to effective treatment. Many times it is important to train caregivers in specific strategies and techniques that promote the transfer of gains from individual therapy into everyday life. The clinician will use a variety of approaches to assist in caregiver training, including: verbal instruction, written direction, modeling, role-play, etc. In addition, family therapy allows the clinician to work with the family as a unit to address issues of concern. Family therapy helps increase appropriate communication while decreasing negative interactions that impact the entire family.

School Consults

Our clinicians frequently complete school observations, consultations with school staff and attend school-based team meetings. Sometimes students demonstrate challenging behaviors and academic difficulties that do not improve regardless of numerous attempts to create interventions in the school setting. Our clinicians specialize in completing objective observations and in working with school staff to address persistent concerns.
Sometimes, school consultations are necessary for the generalization of treatment gains seen in the home and office settings. We frequently find teachers and school staff are eager and willing to collaborate with our professionals. Collaboration allows for the clinician to gain insight into school successes and challenges. In addition, school staff benefit from learning effective treatment methods and strategies through collaboration.
Attending team meetings is an important part of collaboration with school staff. Depending on the need, our professionals participate in informal as well as formal school meetings. For example, sometimes our clinicians participate in regularly scheduled classroom team meetings. With other clients, we may participate in bi-annual or annual formal meetings (ex., IEP and 504 meetings).

Consultations with Other Providers

We encourage collaboration with outside professionals. Our professionals are available for case consultations and trainings with other professionals. Children may demonstrate challenges in other settings, including camp, after-care, doctor and dentist offices and during related therapies (ex., speech, occupational, physical therapies). Our clinicians complete off-site visits when appropriate to address challenging behaviors in different settings.
